Key Policies

Guarantee fair and accessible health care--keep the Noosa Hospital

A Queensland Government document I found in August shows that the LNP government plans to close the public services of the Noosa Hospital. This will result in the loss of essential local services, including Emergency and Intensive Care, and mean that critically ill and injured patients will have to endure the long trip to Nambour or Sippy Downs.

The Minister for Health, Lawrence Springborg, responded to this revelation by call me a "very green Green." Apparently that's the best response LNP central scripting could create.

Read more

Allow citizens to generate, use, and trade cheap and abundant solar energy

Queensland is blessed with a world-class supply of solar energy. This falls on us for free and could provide all the power we will ever need for our homes and industries. The Queensland government, however, now seeks to prevent the deployment of further solar, in order to protect its coal-fired electricity generators.

You can generate all the electricity you need at a cost of about 10c per kilowatt hour, but the government wants to force you to pay more than three times this amount.

Read more

Stop wasting water to burn coal

Operating any of the old-fashioned power plants (coal, gas, nuclear) consumes a vast amount of water. This is the same scarce water which could be used for productive agriculture, urban requirements, or free-flowing rivers and streams.

Read more

Support small business to deliver jobs

Small business is the backbone of the Australian economy and delivers virtually all of the new jobs. But but the LNP and Labor devote all of their policy to the requirements of big business and big projects. This misplaced focus wastes taxpayer money and fails to produce the jobs we require.

Read more

Deliver value to the voters in the seat of Noosa

The cost of running the Queensland's Legislative Assembly is about $1 million per local member per year. Are you getting your money's worth?

Read more

End wasteful and costly subsidies to the coal industry

The Queensland Government is bizarrely committed to subsidising the coal industry. But coal royalties received by the state add up to LESS than the state spends propping up this dinosaur industry.

Read more

Prepare Queensland for an abundant, clean, and prosperous future

Queensland is currently burdened by needlessly high electricity prices. This will cause pain and disruption as people generate their own electricity and many leave the grid altogether, but it looks like we have to go through this phase in order to come out the other side.

The near future (now, if you choose) is one in which households and industry can generate as much electricity as they want, at a much lower cost. But the state has to get out of the way.

Enrol to Vote

You are eligible to vote if you're now 18 or you will be 18 on the next polling day. The next Queensland election is likely to be in March, 2015, but it could be much earlier than that. If you will turn 18 between now and March, you should enrol today so that you will be eligible to vote from your next birthday onward.

Not sure if you are enrolled? Check your enrolment online.
