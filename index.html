Buck Shlegeris

I'm a computer scientist, musician, and aspiring effective altruist from Australia, currently living in San Francisco.

I spent the first half of 2014 in San Francisco, working at App Academy, a coding bootcamp. I'm currently the first and only employee at Triplebyte, a company which is working on improving the interview process for software engineers.

I make fun music like this.

Background

I studied computer science and physics at the Australian National University for two years. At the end of 2013, after completing enough courses to get a computer science major, I moved to San Francisco for App Academy. After the nine week course, App Academy offered me a job teaching the course I'd just done. I worked there until returning to Australia for university in June. You can read an interview I did about App Academy here.

Now, I'm in SF after finishing my degree. I worked for PayPal in Scala, then left to work at Triplebyte.

Programming

My favorite parts of computer science have shifted a lot over the years. I'm really excited about front end web development at the moment, but in the past I've been more interested in data structures. I also love functional programming, programming language implementation, and computer systems stuff.

Effective altruism

I want to use my career to help the world. I am currently trying to do that by maximising my income and then donating as much money as I can. This strategy is known as earning to give.

I'm part of the effective altruism movement, which has sprung up in recent years around organizations like Giving What We Can (of which I am a member), GiveWell, and the Center for Effective Altruism.

I regularly attend the Stanford effective altruism meetups.

I was on a panel at EA Global in Mountain View in 2015.
