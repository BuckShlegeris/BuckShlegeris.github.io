I'm a computer scientist, musician, and effective altruist from Australia, currently living in San Francisco.

I spent the first half of 2014 in San Francisco, working at App Academy, a coding bootcamp. I currently work as a software engineer at Paypal in SOMA, building distributed systems in Scala.

Background

I studied computer science and physics at the Australian National University for two years. At the end of 2013, after completing enough courses to get a computer science major, I moved to San Francisco for App Academy. After the nine week course, App Academy offered me a job teaching the course I'd just done. I worked there until returning to Australia for university in June. You can read an interview I did about App Academy here.

Now, I'm in SF after finishing my degree. I work for PayPal in Scala.

Effective altruism

I want to use my career to help the world. I am currently trying to do that by maximising my income and then donating as much money as I can. This strategy is known as earning to give.

I'm part of the Effective Altruist movement, which has sprung up in recent years around organizations like Giving What We Can (of which I am a member), Givewell, and the Center for Effective Altruism.

You can see many of my thoughts about this on my blog.

I was on a panel at EA Global in Mountain View in 2015.
