Key Policies

ECONOMY: free small business to create jobs, reduce state debt

Queensland's debt is over $16,000 per person, the highest of all states. This is after many years of booming mining activity. The state with the second-highest debt per capita is Western Australia.

But wait a minute--Queensland and WA enjoyed a huge surge in mining activity over the past decade, right? Why do these two states have nothing to show for all of that activity except for a giant debt burden?

Here's the answer: the mining companies take almost all of the profit offshore while leaving the infrastructure costs, the environmental destruction, and the clean-up costs to the government.

Both of the old-fashioned parties are stuck in the past, imagining that big business and big projects will somehow come to the rescue. Apart from consultancies and directorships for retired politicians, the mega-projects have nothing to offer to Queensland.

All of the new jobs in Queensland and especially in the seat of Noosa will come from small business.

EMPLOYMENT: create more local jobs

Small businesses create virtually all of the new jobs in Queensland.

I have owned and operated businesses in Queensland and I have served as a director and advisor to many more. Here in Noosa I serve as a volunteer mentor with the Business Mentors Noosa and work with the proprietors of several small businesses.

I understand the issues faced by small business. Two of the biggest are payroll tax and stamp duty, both of which actively destroy jobs. Neither Labor nor the LNP can address these issues because they’re both beholden to big business. Big business extracts other concessions from government to offset the inconvenience of stamp duty and payroll tax. Small business deserves real support.

ENVIRONMENT: protect and improve our natural abundance

The best economic policies are perfectly compatible with the best environmental policies.

Queensland’s solar energy resource is gigantic. Look beyond the mismanagement of the present electricity system and you can see a time in which electricity is abundant, clean, and cheap. Households and businesses can already save a bundle by generating their own electricity. This on-site generation will increase dramatically once the state gets out of the way.

Queensland could choose to be a major producer of aluminium or magnesium, for example, using abundant and cheap solar electricity. Such materials are themselves important for energy-saving advances in transport and other industries. Queensland can choose to start this virtuous, job-creating cycle. The first step is to vote Green.

ENERGY: enable clean, cheap electricity; free the access to the grid

We must eliminate Queensland’s giant new tax on electricity users.

Queensland’s electricity system has entered its death spiral. As prices rise faster and faster, people find ways to use less. This means the price has to rise even faster to keep the electricity generators from going broke.

The current government’s desperate ploy to delay the inevitable is to increase every household’s fixed charge. That’s the fee you pay just for being connected, rather than for the electricity you use. This tax/fee has increased by $227 for every single connection in just the past two years. This is a great big new tax on every Queenslander which has no purpose other than papering over the mismanagement of the electricity system by successive governments on both sides.

Queensland’s existing grid could have great value, delivering low-cost, clean energy from a vast and competitive array of private suppliers. This need not cost the state anything, as the private sector is busting to install large-scale solar and wind capacity. The state just needs to get out of the way.

EQUITY: stop wasteful subsidies to mining giants

The Greens do not oppose mining for sound, economically productive purposes. We do oppose wasteful subsidies.

The Queensland government spends more on subsidies to the mining industry than it collects in royalties. This has to stop.

ENHANCE and EXPAND health services--keep the Noosa Hospital

We must maintain and expand the services offered at the Noosa Hospital. The state must commit long-term funding for public beds, the emergency department, and the intensive care facility. If the state government regards Noosa as a safe seat it will proceed with its plans to gut and then shut our most important public health facility. A vote for the Greens offers your best chance to keep and enhance essential hospital services here in Noosa.
